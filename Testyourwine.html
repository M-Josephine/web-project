Let's test your wine!

Based on physicochemical properties and machine learning, we will give you an idea of your wine's quality.

Enter the properties

To train the model of machine learning, I used a dataset from Kaggle, based on physicochemical tests (input variables) and a sensory variable (score between 0 and 10). I set a cutoff between good quality wine (score >= 7) and low quality wine (score < 7). To predict the quality, I used two classifiers, KNN and Decision Tree and tuned the hyperparameters with GridSeachCV.

References: P. Cortez, A. Cerdeira, F. Almeida, T. Matos and J. Reis. Modeling wine preferences by data mining from physicochemical properties. In Decision Support Systems, Elsevier, 47(4):547-553, 2009.
